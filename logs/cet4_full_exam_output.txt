**A**  
The digital age has transformed the way we communicate, work, and live. With the rise of smartphones, social media, and instant messaging, people are more connected than ever before. However, this constant connectivity has also led to concerns about privacy, mental health, and the quality of interpersonal relationships. While technology offers convenience, it also raises questions about its long-term effects on society.  

**B**  
One of the most significant impacts of technology is on the workplace. Remote work, enabled by digital tools, has become increasingly common. Employees can now collaborate across time zones without being physically present in an office. This shift has improved work-life balance for many but has also blurred the boundaries between professional and personal time. Some argue that this flexibility is beneficial, while others worry about burnout and decreased productivity.  

**C**  
Education is another area deeply affected by technology. Online learning platforms have made education more accessible to people worldwide. Students can now attend lectures, submit assignments, and even earn degrees without setting foot in a classroom. Despite these advantages, critics point out that online education lacks the social interaction and hands-on experiences traditional schools provide. The debate continues over whether digital learning can fully replace in-person instruction.  

**D**  
The entertainment industry has also undergone a massive transformation due to technology. Streaming services like Netflix and Spotify have replaced traditional TV and radio, allowing users to consume content on demand. This shift has given consumers more control over what they watch and listen to but has also raised concerns about the decline of live events and the oversaturation of content.  

**E**  
Healthcare is another sector where technology has made a profound impact. Telemedicine allows patients to consult doctors remotely, reducing the need for in-person visits. Wearable devices like smartwatches can monitor vital signs and detect potential health issues early. These advancements have improved access to care, especially in rural areas, but they also pose challenges related to data security and the accuracy of digital diagnoses.  

**F**  
Despite its benefits, technology has also contributed to growing issues like cybercrime and misinformation. Hackers exploit vulnerabilities in digital systems to steal sensitive information, while fake news spreads rapidly on social media platforms. Governments and organizations are struggling to keep up with these threats, highlighting the need for stronger regulations and cybersecurity measures.  

**G**  
The environmental impact of technology is another pressing concern. The production and disposal of electronic devices contribute to pollution and resource depletion. While some companies are adopting sustainable practices, such as recycling programs and energy-efficient designs, the tech industry still has a long way to go in reducing its carbon footprint.  

**H**  
On a personal level, technology has changed how people form and maintain relationships. Social media allows individuals to stay in touch with friends and family across the globe, but it can also lead to superficial connections and reduced face-to-face interactions. Studies suggest that excessive screen time may contribute to feelings of loneliness and anxiety, especially among younger generations.  

**I**  
The future of technology holds both promise and uncertainty. Innovations like artificial intelligence and virtual reality could revolutionize industries, but they also raise ethical questions about job displacement and privacy. As society continues to adapt to these changes, it will be crucial to balance progress with responsibility.  

**J**  
Governments and policymakers play a key role in shaping the direction of technological development. Regulations are needed to ensure that innovations benefit society without causing harm. For example, laws governing data privacy and AI ethics are essential to protect individuals and prevent misuse.  

**K**  
Individuals also have a responsibility to use technology mindfully. Setting boundaries, such as limiting screen time and being cautious about sharing personal information online, can help mitigate some of the negative effects. Education about digital literacy is equally important to empower people to navigate the digital world safely.  

**L**  
Ultimately, technology is a tool that reflects the values and priorities of those who use it. While it has the potential to solve global challenges, its impact depends on how it is developed and implemented. Society must work together to harness its benefits while addressing its drawbacks.  

**M**  
In conclusion, the digital age has brought unprecedented opportunities and challenges. From transforming industries to altering personal relationships, technology’s influence is undeniable. As we move forward, finding a balance between innovation and responsibility will be key to ensuring a positive future for all.

**CET-4 Close Reading Passage 1**  

The rise of social media has dramatically changed how people communicate, share information, and perceive the world. Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram allow users to connect with others instantly, regardless of geographical barriers. While these platforms offer convenience and entertainment, they also raise concerns about privacy, mental health, and the spread of misinformation.  

One major issue is the impact of social media on mental well-being. Studies suggest that excessive use can lead to anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem, particularly among young people. Constant exposure to idealized versions of others' lives may create unrealistic expectations, causing users to feel inadequate. Additionally, the addictive nature of these platforms, driven by algorithms designed to maximize engagement, makes it difficult for individuals to disconnect.  

Another concern is the spread of false information. Social media enables rapid dissemination of content, but without proper fact-checking, misleading or outright false stories can go viral. This phenomenon, known as "fake news," has influenced public opinion on critical issues like elections and health crises. Despite efforts by platforms to flag or remove inaccurate content, the sheer volume of posts makes it challenging to control.  

Privacy is also a significant problem. Many users unknowingly share personal data, which companies may use for targeted advertising or sell to third parties. While some platforms offer privacy settings, the complexity of these options often leaves users vulnerable to data breaches or unwanted surveillance.  

Despite these drawbacks, social media remains a powerful tool for education, activism, and community building. It has played a key role in organizing social movements, such as climate change protests and humanitarian campaigns. The challenge lies in finding a balance between harnessing its benefits and mitigating its risks.

**CET-4 Close Reading Passage 2**  

**The Rise of Telecommuting**  

In recent years, telecommuting—working remotely from home or another location—has become increasingly popular. Advances in technology, such as high-speed internet and video conferencing tools, have made it easier for employees to perform their jobs outside traditional office spaces. Companies are also recognizing the benefits of telecommuting, including reduced overhead costs and access to a wider talent pool. However, this shift is not without challenges.  

One major advantage of telecommuting is flexibility. Employees can often set their own schedules, allowing them to balance work and personal life more effectively. Studies show that remote workers report higher job satisfaction and lower stress levels. Additionally, companies save money on office space, utilities, and other expenses. Some businesses have even adopted a hybrid model, where employees split their time between home and the office.  

Despite these benefits, telecommuting presents difficulties. Communication can be less efficient without face-to-face interaction, and some employees struggle with isolation. Managers may find it harder to monitor productivity, and team cohesion can suffer. Furthermore, not all jobs are suitable for remote work, particularly those requiring physical presence or specialized equipment.  

As telecommuting continues to grow, both employers and employees must adapt. Clear communication, structured routines, and reliable technology are essential for success. While it may not replace traditional offices entirely, telecommuting is likely to remain a significant part of the modern work landscape.

中国书法是中国传统文化的重要组成部分，被誉为“无言的诗，无形的舞”。它不仅是文字的书写艺术，更是一种修身养性的方式。书法家通过毛笔、墨汁和宣纸，将汉字的美感与个人情感融为一体。每一笔、每一划都讲究力度与节奏，体现了“天人合一”的哲学思想。从甲骨文到楷书，中国书法历经数千年演变，形成了独特的艺术体系。如今，书法不仅是中国人日常生活中的高雅爱好，也成为世界了解中国文化的窗口。许多外国友人通过学习书法，感受中华文明的深厚底蕴。
